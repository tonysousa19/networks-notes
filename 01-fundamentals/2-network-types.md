# Network Types

## Definition

Network types describe how networks are organized based on their size, coverage area, and purpose.

Different network types are used depending on the distance between devices and the requirements of communication.

## LAN (Local Area Network)

A LAN connects devices within a small geographic area.

Examples:

- Home networks
- Schools
- Offices
- Small businesses

Characteristics:

- High speed
- Low latency
- Usually privately managed
- Uses Ethernet or Wi-Fi

Example:

A company connecting all computers inside one building.

---

## WAN (Wide Area Network)

A WAN connects networks over large geographic distances.

Examples:

- The Internet
- Company branches in different cities
- Global networks

Characteristics:

- Covers large areas
- Uses multiple communication technologies
- Usually depends on service providers

Example:

A company connecting offices in Brazil, Canada, and Germany.

---

## MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)

A MAN covers a larger area than a LAN but smaller than a WAN.

It usually connects networks within a city or metropolitan region.

Examples:

- City-wide internet providers
- University campuses
- Government networks

Characteristics:

- Medium geographic range
- Often uses fiber optic connections

---

## PAN (Personal Area Network)

A PAN connects devices around a single person.

Examples:

- Smartphone and smartwatch
- Bluetooth headphones
- Personal devices

Characteristics:

- Very short range
- Usually wireless

---

## WLAN (Wireless Local Area Network)

A WLAN is a LAN that uses wireless communication.

The most common technology is Wi-Fi.

Examples:

- Home Wi-Fi
- Office wireless networks

Characteristics:

- Uses radio signals
- Allows device mobility

---

## CAN (Campus Area Network)

A CAN connects multiple LANs inside a limited campus or organization.

Examples:

- Universities
- Military bases
- Large companies

Characteristics:

- Larger than LAN
- Smaller than MAN

---

## VPN (Virtual Private Network)

A VPN creates a secure connection over an existing network.

It allows users to securely access resources from another location.

Examples:

- Remote workers accessing company systems
- Secure connections over public Wi-Fi

Characteristics:

- Encrypts traffic
- Protects data from interception

---

## Network Type Comparison

| Type | Coverage | Example |
|---|---|---|
| PAN | Few meters | Bluetooth devices |
| LAN | Building or home | Office network |
| CAN | Campus | University network |
| MAN | City | City network |
| WAN | Global | Internet |

---

## Cybersecurity Importance

Understanding network types helps security professionals identify:

- Possible attack surfaces
- Network boundaries
- Trust zones
- Where security controls should be applied

Knowing the size and structure of a network is important for monitoring, defense, and incident response.
